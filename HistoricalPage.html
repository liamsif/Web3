Historical Events

• In late 1999, while the towers were not yet inhabited, a countdown to the top of the Triangle Tower was conducted towards the year 2000, using digits and giant letters created by computer-controlled lighting. Since then, the towers have served as a platform for presenting various messages to the general public, through lighting design designed for them. Among other things, they were presented with a project to raise national morale, and every year on Independence Day they are lit in the shape of the Israeli flag.

• In 2003, the towers began a local tradition of - "leap uphill", a stair race in which competitors climb up 1,144 steps in the round tower to the helipad at the top.

• The Israel Sports Navigation Association held park navigation events in the shopping center and underground parking lot below. In 2006 a tradition of a series of spinning classes began on the helicopter landing pad at the top of the round tower. In 2007 there were 24 lessons in a row, which started on Thursday afternoon and ended before the beginning of Shabbat.
